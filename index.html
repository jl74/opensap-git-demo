Devil Facts

The Devil (from Greek: διάβολος diábolos "slanderer, accuser") is the personification and archetype of evil in various cultures. Historically, the Devil can be defined as the personification of whatever is perceived in society as evil and the depiction consist of its cultural traditions.

  • In monistic view, God is absolute embracing both good and evil.
  • In the Mitigated dualism, the Devil is thought as a creature, somehow fell apart from good, thus giving existence to evil.
  • Christianity identifies the Devil ("Satan") with the serpent who tempted Adam and Eve to eat the forbidden fruit.
  • The Islamic equivalent of Satan is named Iblis.
